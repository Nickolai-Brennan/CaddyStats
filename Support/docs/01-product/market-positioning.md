# Market Positioning

Last updated: 2026-05-12
Owner: Product Marketing
Status: Working draft

## Positioning Statement

For serious golf bettors and fantasy players who need trustworthy, decision-ready analysis, Caddy Stats is a data-first golf intelligence platform that combines explainable projections, betting workflows, and structured editorial content. Unlike generic pick sites or disconnected stat tools, Caddy Stats grounds every insight in verifiable data and transparent model logic.

## Category Definition

Caddy Stats competes in an emerging category:

Golf Analytics and Betting Intelligence Platform

Core category attributes:

- Verifiable data foundations
- Projection-driven decision support
- Clear model explainability
- Workflow speed from discovery to shortlist
- Consistent free-to-premium value ladder

## Ideal Customer Segments

- Segment A: Serious golf bettors with recurring bankroll allocation
- Segment B: DFS/fantasy users who need projection and leverage context
- Segment C: Data-oriented golf fans seeking transparent analysis

## Problem We Solve

Users currently stitch together fragmented tools and low-trust content to make weekly decisions. Caddy Stats reduces fragmentation and improves confidence by unifying stats, projections, and action-oriented research workflows.

## Differentiation Pillars

1. Explainable projections:
Recommendations are accompanied by model context, not just rankings.

2. Unified decision workflow:
Users can move across outrights, placements, matchups, and DFS in one system.

3. Grounded editorial engine:
Content scales without abandoning data quality or trust.

4. Transparent quality posture:
Model versioning and retrospective performance reporting are first-class.

## Messaging Framework

### Core Promise

Smarter golf decisions, grounded in data you can inspect.

### Supporting Messages

- See not just who is ranked, but why.
- Move faster from research to actionable shortlist.
- Separate signal from noise with projection-backed workflows.
- Use one platform for analytics, betting intelligence, and research content.

### Proof Elements

- Structured stats and projection sources
- Explainability modules
- Course, player, and tournament integrated pages
- Versioned models and quality dashboards

## Competitive Position Map

Axes:

- X-axis: Explainability depth
- Y-axis: Workflow utility for weekly decisions

Target position:
Top-right quadrant (high explainability + high workflow utility).

## Pricing Position

Position as premium-but-practical:

- More rigorous than commodity pick subscriptions
- More decision-focused than broad stat repositories
- Priced for recurring weekly utility rather than one-off content consumption

## Tone and Brand Voice

- Analytical but readable
- Confident but non-hyperbolic
- Transparent about uncertainty
- Avoid guarantee language

## Claims Governance

All external claims must satisfy:

- Traceable to data source or internal computation
- Distinction between measured value and model estimate
- Explicit caveats where uncertainty is material

## Positioning Risks

- Drifting toward generic pick content dilutes trust
- Overly technical language can reduce conversion for newer users
- Weak free tier utility can suppress acquisition

## Quarterly Positioning Validation

Review these questions each quarter:

1. Are users describing the product with the intended category language?
2. Are conversion rates higher for users exposed to explainability modules?
3. Are we preserving trust while increasing publishing velocity?
4. Do premium users perceive clear value beyond free alternatives?
