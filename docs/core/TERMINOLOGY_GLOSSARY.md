# Terminology Glossary

## Product and business terms

- **Caddy Stats**: the golf analytics and betting intelligence product in this repository.
- **Strik3Zone**: the parent brand for Caddy Stats.
- **Anonymous user**: a visitor with no authenticated account.
- **Member**: a registered user with limited free or onboarding entitlements.
- **Subscriber**: a paying user with premium feature access.
- **Affiliate value**: revenue generated when trusted research surfaces drive qualified partner referrals.

## Data and modeling terms

- **Canonical entity**: the source-of-truth representation of a player, tournament, course, market, or content object.
- **Grounding**: the process of tying an output or claim back to verified data or explicit derived logic.
- **Model run**: one execution of a projection or analytics pipeline with versioned inputs and outputs.
- **Projection**: a model-derived expectation for a player, tournament outcome, or market context.
- **Freshness**: the timestamped recency signal attached to ingested or derived data.
- **Materialized view**: a precomputed read model used for high-read analytics paths.

## Editorial and SEO terms

- **Structured block**: a typed content unit used to compose articles or templates safely.
- **Editorial intelligence**: the operating model that combines structured content, analytics, and AI assistance for publishing.
- **Programmatic content**: repeatable public pages generated from verified entities and templates.
- **Premium editorial**: subscriber-oriented narrative or research that extends beyond free discovery content.

## Platform and architecture terms

- **Bounded context**: a domain boundary with its own language, ownership, and persistence rules.
- **Service boundary**: the operational contract that defines what a runtime service owns and may call.
- **Entitlement**: a capability grant derived from subscription state or administrative access.
- **ADR**: an Architectural Decision Record that captures a durable architecture or governance choice.
- **Event-driven architecture**: the use of emitted domain or integration events to trigger downstream processing asynchronously.

## Governance terms

- **Canonical docs**: the documentation set under `docs/` that defines current product and system intent.
- **Legacy source material**: older planning or support docs retained for reference, especially under `docs/legacy/support/`.
- **Release class**: patch, minor, or major classification used to describe change impact.
