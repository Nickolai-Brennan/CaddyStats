# AI Strategy

## Objective

Use AI to accelerate editorial and analytical workflows without allowing generated output to override verified data, product controls, or human review requirements.

## Approved use cases

- grounded draft assistance for editorial workflows
- summarization of verified statistics or model outputs
- internal analysis support for model review and content preparation
- operational copilots that work from explicit, approved context

## Non-approved use cases

- inventing player stats, lines, injuries, or field changes
- publishing unreviewed betting claims as facts
- bypassing entitlements, audit logging, or editorial approval paths
- silently rewriting canonical source data

## Control model

- prompts and workflow versions must be documented or logged
- every sensitive AI-assisted output must retain source references or grounding status
- human review is required for betting, projection, or subscriber-impacting narratives
- failures to ground or validate data should block publication, not be papered over

## Product implication

AI is a force multiplier for the product, not the product's truth source. Trust still comes from verified data, transparent methodology, and accountable editorial or operational review.
